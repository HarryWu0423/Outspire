What kind of holographic effect?

Each reacts to device tilt — the difference is what moves and how it looks

Period 3 — Mathematics
Room 302 · 25 min remaining

A. Gradient Shift

The card's existing gradient colors smoothly shift their angle/position as you tilt. Like a pearl or oil-on-water — the colors themselves move. Subtle and integrated with your existing coloredRichCard style.

B. Specular Highlight

A soft, blurred light "hotspot" moves across the card surface as you tilt — like light reflecting off a glossy surface. The card colors stay fixed; only the light moves. Think Apple's credit card UI or a polished metal surface.

C. Iridescent Rainbow

A faint rainbow spectrum sweeps across the card as you tilt — like a holographic trading card or credit card hologram. Most "holographic" of the three, but needs careful opacity to stay subtle.

D. Gradient Shift + Specular (Hybrid)

Combines A and B: the gradient angle shifts with tilt AND a soft light hotspot moves across. Richer than either alone, still avoids the full rainbow. My recommendation — most premium feel without being flashy.
